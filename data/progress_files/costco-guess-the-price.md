# Guess the Price — Costco Pack (40 items)

**As-of date:** 2026-07-12 · **US prices** · Sorted low → high for the game master.

Costco doesn't publish warehouse/food-court prices on costco.com, and many
big-ticket items hide their price behind a member login. So sources are tiered:
- **Food court & flat-price staples** → news/CEO statements (hot dog, chicken).
- **Warehouse-only Kirkland groceries** → WarehouseRunner, an in-store price
  tracker aggregating shelf-tag scans across 600+ warehouses (best available
  single-number source; brand/Costco.com don't list these).
- **Big-ticket** → Costco.com product pages where a clean number is public.

Every price below is a single clean number confirmed as of 2026-07-12. Items
that move (sold by weight, seasonal, or promo) are called out in **Notes/flags**.

## Notes / flags

**Sold by weight / not a fixed SKU price (price is typical, will drift):**
- **#12 Deli mac & cheese ($13.09)** — priced at $3.49/lb; a tray is ~3.8 lb, so
  the total ranges roughly $12–$15. I used $13.09 (Costco Guides). If you want a
  hard fixed number, swap for **Kirkland chicken bake, $3.99** (flat food-court
  price) — but that's a 3rd food-court item, so it'd nudge the category cap.
- **#14 Parmigiano Reggiano ($14.79/lb)** — random-weight wedges (~1.5–2 lb), so
  a wedge rings up ~$22–$30. Price shown is per pound. Fine for the game if you
  say "per pound"; otherwise swap for a fixed SKU.

**Seasonal / promo drift (verified today, but bounce more than shelf staples):**
- **#22 Milk chocolate almonds ($18.99)** and **#15 crispy wings ($14.99)** —
  both are current post-cut prices Costco's CFO confirmed on the Q3-FY26 call
  (May 28, 2026). Solid, but these were just changed.
- **#32 Wild sockeye salmon ($42.99)** — seasonal; range seen $39.99–$42.99
  (avg $42.88). Farmed Atlantic salmon 3 lb is a cheaper cousin (~$38–$48).
- **#33 Roomba j8+ ($499.99)** — Costco shows member-only pricing (login-gated);
  $499.99 is the widely-reported current online+warehouse price, but it has
  swung $399.97–$599.99 on promos. Most volatile item on the list. If you want a
  rock-steady electronics slot, this is the one to watch or swap.
- **#26 Men's shirt jacket ($19.99)** — apparel is warehouse-only and clears out;
  $19.99 is the regular online price (some warehouses mark it down to ~$5).

**Big-ticket price is warehouse vs online:**
- **#35 Weber grill** and **#36 Callaway golf set** — warehouse runs ~$30–$50
  cheaper than the Costco.com/Costco Direct number ($549.99 vs $599.99 grill;
  $599.99 vs $629.99 golf set). I used the figures most consistently reported.
- **#39/#40 diamonds** — Costco lists several variants; I picked the classic
  1 ctw solitaire studs and the 5 ctw tennis bracelet. Costco.com carries a
  cheaper ~$999.99 stud variant and 1–8 ctw bracelets if you want to re-band.

**Couldn't pin a clean single US number → deliberately excluded (offer as swaps):**
- **Dual-fuel generator** — Costco.com shows "for item price, visit your local
  warehouse" (no online number). Verified swap already in: none needed.
- **65" TV** — Costco.com hides logged-out pricing; no clean public US number.
  Verified electronics swap used instead: **Roomba j8+ ($499.99)**.
- **Michelin Defender 2 tires** — priced by vehicle fitment ($130–$220+/tire),
  never a single clean number at Costco. Excluded.

**Optional pushback on the brief:** you asked to mix in luxury big-ticket items,
which I did (pergola, diamonds, golf set, grill, mattress). Because Costco's
top-end jewelry runs into the thousands, the price ladder is long ($1.50 →
$4,000). For a party game that's great drama, but if players anchor on food and
lowball everything, the two $2k–$4k jewelry items can feel unguessable. If you'd
rather a tighter, more "guessable" spread, cap the top around the pergola
($1,999.99) and drop the tennis bracelet (#40), backfilling with e.g. a
**Kirkland cashew clusters** or **Kirkland Prosecco** for a mid-band item.
